Set up your tank

Your choices will affect how the cycle unfolds — and what to expect along the way.

Tank size
🫙 Small Under 40L · Less forgiving
🪣 Medium 40–120L · More stable
🏊 Large 120L+ · Most forgiving
Cycling method
🧪 Fishless Add ammonia source, no fish harmed
🐠 Fish-in Traditional but harder on fish

Fishless cycling is recommended. Adding pure ammonia or a piece of food — rather than live fish — lets the bacteria establish without putting any animals through the stress of high ammonia. It is gentler, more controllable, and produces the same result.

Fish-in cycling places real stress on fish. Ammonia and nitrite will rise before bacteria can process them. If you use this method, very small fish populations, frequent small water changes, and close observation are essential. Many keepers now prefer fishless cycling for this reason.

Starting plants?
🌿 Yes Helps buffer nitrate
— No plants Less buffering

With a small tank, fishless cycling, and plants — expect the cycle to complete in roughly 3–4 weeks. Ammonia will peak first, then nitrite, then both will fall as bacteria establish. Plants will help keep nitrate manageable.
